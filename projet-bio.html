"Its's to the ancient art of Bonsai," explains bonn O'brien between sips of house blend at brooklyn coffe sho^p. "It takes patience. It takes an eye for detail. It's reverence, respect and a steady hand all rolled into one. It's the only way to take care of proper beard. We take the sam approach to our music."

Three years ago, the O'brien-lead The Dapper beards burst into the country's collective consciousness like a Salvador Dali'stache thanks to their now-iconic appearance on the cover of the rendy periodical "Garden and Gun". "The Beards Are Here,And They Will Save Folk Music" read the headline. The striking imageof the four bandmates in repose, silhouetted by a Soho sunset was an immediate viral sensation.

"We used", says O'brien with the twist of this mustache, "that's how it went viral. I don't think anyone had seen a bolk a bold move like that-a band, suing to protect their their image aft signing off on the cover. We owe a big thanks to the Smoking gun for making that happen. We had approved the cover, but we hadn't approved of the people who were going to be reading the magazine. Turns out they didn't approach that story with the kind of reverence with wich we treat our beards-the same approach we take to our music-and we didn't appreciate that."

And the Folk pigeonhole? O'brien didn't take kindly to that, either.

"They never took the time to ask for the spelling. It may sound like the word that is commonly used to describe some kinds of roots music, but it wasn't the word that we were using. it's not even the music we're doing. Folk-and that's the only time i'm going to use that word-is so over. We're Fauxlk. Pronounced the same way as folk-wich, really, is the last time i'm going to use that word-but it's spelled F-a-u-x-l-k. if you're not going to to ask us about our music, which we take great care in- similar to the way in wich we care for our beards-how can we expect you to represent our movement in its proper context to your readers?
